Cryo-EM: Overfocus & Underfocus in TEM

How objective lens strength affects phase contrast imaging · 300 kV · Cs = 2.7 mm

OVERFOCUSΔf = −0.5 μm
Simulated Image
Bright fringe outside object edge. Contrast inverted vs underfocus.
  • Lens too strong (f↓) — image plane forms above detector
  • Electrons cross over before detector, then diverge
  • Fresnel fringe on outside of edges (white halo)
  • CTF sign reverses → contrast inversion
  • Rarely used in cryo-EM; verifies focus direction
IN FOCUSΔf = 0
Simulated Image
Nearly blank — weak phase objects produce almost no amplitude contrast.
  • Exact focus — image plane = detector
  • Phase shifts do not convert to amplitude contrast
  • No Fresnel fringes visible
  • CTF = −sin(χ) = 0 when Δf = 0
  • Highest resolution but no contrast for biological samples
UNDERFOCUSΔf = +1.0 μm
Simulated Image
Dark fringe inside object edge. Standard cryo-EM condition.
  • Lens too weak (f↑) — image plane forms below detector
  • Electrons not yet converged at detector
  • Fresnel fringe on inside of edges (dark halo)
  • Phase → amplitude conversion enables structure visibility
  • Standard: 0.5–1.5 μm underfocus for cryo-EM
