Effect of Objective Lens Strength on TEM Image Formation

Δf = defocus (image plane ↔ detector distance) · BFP = Back Focal Plane

OVERFOCUS
Lens too strong (f↓)
  • Image plane forms above detector
  • Electrons cross over, then diverge at detector
  • Fresnel fringe on outside of edges (bright halo)
  • CTF sign reversal → contrast inversion
IN FOCUS
Exact focus
  • Image plane = detector plane
  • Phase objects produce no amplitude contrast
  • No Fresnel fringes visible
  • Highest resolution but specimen invisible
UNDERFOCUS
Lens too weak (f↑)
  • Image plane forms below detector
  • Electrons not yet converged at detector
  • Fresnel fringe on inside of edges (dark halo)
  • Standard cryo-EM: 0.5–1.5 μm underfocus
